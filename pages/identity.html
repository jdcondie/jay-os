Identity & Ownership
Your brain executes when responsibility becomes identity, not obligation.

The Ownership Principle

Before: "I should help with the dog." → After: "This is my dog." Once ownership flipped, follow-through became automatic. Nothing about the task changed. The category changed.

Obligation vs Ownership

OBLIGATION

"I should do this" → browser tabs stay open. Brain simulates alternatives. Cognitive friction everywhere.

OWNERSHIP

"This is mine." → Starting gun fires. Only option is forward. Follow-through becomes automatic.

The Ownership Test

Run before any task that keeps getting displaced.

1
Is this mine — or am I "helping"? If helping, reframe: "This deliverable is my reputation."
2
Can I find the cathedral? What am I building by doing this? If the answer is just "getting it done," dig deeper.
3
Would I be proud of this? What would I do differently if this were mine to win with?
4
Can I make this identity? Not "I have to send this email" — "I'm the person who delivers clean work fast."

If you can't pass any of these, the task may not be yours. Delegate, automate, or renegotiate.
