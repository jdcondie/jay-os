Rules of Thumb
The operating principles that don't change.
Ownership creates follow-through. Tasks become automatic when responsibility becomes identity. "I should help" → "This is mine." That switch is everything.
Constraint beats willpower every time. Make the wrong option harder to reach, not the right one more appealing. Friction is the variable. Not discipline.
Finishing requires structure. Builders need systems to close loops. The finish line is another build phase — frame it that way.
Single visible task improves focus. Removing alternatives reduces drift. When the only option is forward, the brain doesn't wander.
Shame destroys hardware. Cortisol is toxic to the prefrontal cortex. You cannot shame yourself into sustained productivity. Coach B is a biological necessity.
Creation replenishes energy. Even small. Especially when stuck. Creation produces dopamine — the gatekeeper for your TPN.
Environment beats discipline. Design the space before needing willpower. Light, gaze, space, and digital constraints do the work discipline cannot.
Emotion first, task second. Motivation blocks are emotional, not moral. Process the feeling before forcing action. Avoidance is almost always emotional avoidance in disguise.
Small reps beat intensity. Consistent tiny steps accumulate more reliably than sporadic large pushes. The start is the hardest part — make it tiny.
Motion creates clarity. Act first. Clarity follows action more than it precedes it. "What's the next physical action?" is always the right question.
Past commitment states are clues. The moments of "I will finish no matter what" have appeared before. Study them. Extract the internal condition. Recreate it.
Failure is impossible. Magic is expected. Positive outcomes are inevitable. Decisions made from this belief produce a completely different life than decisions made from fear.
Every protocol in this system does one thing: shrink the optionality until only the real task remains. Not by force. By architecture. Design environments that make the right thing the only thing. That's the whole game.
