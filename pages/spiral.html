Spiral Protocol
Replaying failures. Coach A is running the show.
Shame is destroying the hardware you need. Cortisol is toxic to the prefrontal cortex — the exact region responsible for focus and executive function. You cannot shame yourself into sustained productivity.

Coach A — The Critic

"You can't do anything right."

"You're always like this."

"You'll never follow through."

→ Cortisol. Burns tank. Destroys hardware.

Coach B — The Ally

"Okay. We missed that. Reset."

"What's one thing I can do right now?"

"This feeling is temporary."

→ Dopamine. Rebuilds. Moves forward.

Coach A feels more true because it's louder and older. Loudness is not accuracy.

The Protocol

  • 01Notice it. "That's Coach A. Not me. Not truth. Just the old script." You are not your thoughts. You are the person who notices the thoughts.
  • 02Interrupt it. Say "STOP" out loud. Or a physical trigger — stand up, clap, snap. Break the loop physically.
  • 03Replace it. Ask: "What would Coach B say right now?" Answer it. That's the whole move.
  • 04Reframe the failure: Temporary (this one task doesn't represent total capability). Limited (one problem doesn't define the whole system). Nonrepresentative (admin misses don't reflect real capability).
  • 05Move your body. 20 minutes minimum. Not optional in a spiral.
  • 06Create something. Anything. Shifts brain from emotional processing to system design processing — your natural strength.
  • 07Talk to one trusted person. Verbal processing with a trusted person resets the nervous system.

Anchor Questions

Is this aligned with who I'm becoming — or who I used to be?
Does this expand my life — or shrink it?
Am I acting from faith — or from fear right now?
Failure is impossible. Magic is expected. Positive outcomes are inevitable. Decisions made from this belief produce a completely different life than decisions made from fear.
