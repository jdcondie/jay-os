Decision Filters
Run before you commit, buy in, or say yes.

Daily 60-Second Reset

Once per day. 60-second timer. Short phrases only. Stop when time ends.

10s
Orientation: What am I actually working toward right now?
15s
Alignment: What moved me closer yesterday? What pulled me away?
15s
Avoidance: What decision/convo/action am I avoiding today?
10s
Subtraction: What can I remove/delay/no today?
10s
Action lock: Smallest physical action that reduces mental weight?
Red flags (repeat = reassess): Same avoidance answer 3+ days. No clear action after check-in. Constant urgency with no progress. Adding tasks without subtracting.

Daily Decision Filter

Run top to bottom. Any "no" = do not proceed.

1
Identity: Does this align with who I'm becoming?
2
Priority: Does this serve my top 1–2 outcomes?
3
Cost: What does this quietly cost (time/energy/health/relationships)?
4
Subtraction: What must I remove to do this well?
5
Peace vs validation: Am I choosing alignment or approval?

Opportunity Evaluation

For offers, partnerships, new commitments. Hit a "No" → stop.

1
Clarity: Is this an obvious yes? If not, it's a no.
2
Downside: Can I survive the worst case?
3
Upside: Asymmetric upside — or vanity/maintenance?
4
Freedom: More control over time later — or locked into obligations?
5
Peace of mind: Will future-me feel lighter or heavier?
6
Systemization: Can it become repeatable without constant willpower?
7
Consistency: Am I willing to do the unsexy work consistently?
8
Authenticity: Is this for me — or to impress someone?

Emergency "Stuck" Protocol

When looping. Answer fast. Move immediately after.

1
What decision am I avoiding right now?
2
If nothing changes, where does this lead in 2–3 years?
3
What single action reduces the most mental weight today?
4
What am I doing to stay busy instead of honest?
5
What would I tell a close friend to do — today?
Exit rule: Clarity without action decays. Take the smallest honest step.
