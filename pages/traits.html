Maintain pace and commitment regardless of external success or failure. Consistency doesn't require motivation.

The "Drop Everything and Do It" Rule

"As soon as I decide I want to do something, I take the first step that same day." If you have an idea or goal, immediately take the first action — even if small. Don't wait to plan, overthink, or perfect it. Start moving right away. This rule ensures momentum and avoids paralysis by analysis.

Do What Scares You — The Framework

  • 01Pick something that scares you — but that you deeply want to overcome. It must make you uncomfortable, but also curious or drawn to it.
  • 02Give yourself a time limit. A clear end date with a visible "performance" moment — a deadline that makes it real.
  • 03Hire a coach or find a mentor. You don't do it alone. Seek someone who has already done it. Coaching compresses the timeline.
  • 04Create public or personal stakes. Tell someone. Put it on the calendar. The pressure forces you to confront discomfort instead of deferring it.
  • 05Track growth, not just outcome. Success is defined by the attempt and transformation, not by perfection.
Why it works: breaks fear paralysis, builds confidence through action, and strengthens identity. You become someone who does scary things — not someone who avoids them.
